Methodology & caveats

The circle is a size, not a place. Its area equals the total ecological footprint. The real land that feeds and supplies a city or province is scattered across the whole planet and its oceans, and most of it is nowhere near the dot you clicked. The circle just puts that total area on the map so you can see how big it is.

A global hectare isn't a hectare you can walk across. One gha is a hectare of world-average land productivity. Canadian land tends to be less productive than that average, so the real area on the ground would be bigger than the circle you see.

The per-person number is a national average. Every place here uses Canada's average of 8.1 gha per person unless a note says otherwise. Real footprints move around with income, density, climate, and how people get about, so read these as rough teaching figures rather than exact measurements.
