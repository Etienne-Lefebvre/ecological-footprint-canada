Methodology & caveats

What the circle means. The overlay is a circle whose area equals the city's total ecological footprint. It shows scale, not location: a city's real footprint is scattered across the entire globe and its oceans, not clustered around the city.

Global hectares (gha) ≠ physical hectares. A gha is normalized to world-average biological productivity. Canadian land is generally less productive than the world average, so the true physical area would be larger than the circle drawn here.

Per-capita figure is a national average. Unless noted, each city uses Canada's national average of 8.1 gha/person. Real cities vary with income, density, climate, and transport — treat this as an educational approximation, not a measurement.
